# Hypothesis Evolution: From Neglect to State-Sponsored Crime

This document tracks the evolution of our understanding of the Hungarian Child Protection Crisis as the investigation progressed from standard NGO reports to the deep-dive analysis of internal transcripts and elite networks.

---

## Phase 1: The "Systemic Neglect" Hypothesis
**Source Material:** `docs/COMPREHENSIVE_ANALYSIS_REPORT.md` (NGOs, Ombudsman, EU reports).

*   **The Core Idea:** The system is failing due to incompetence, underfunding, and bad prioritization.
*   **Key Drivers Identified:**
    *   **Austerity:** Low wages causing staff shortages.
    *   **Bureaucracy:** Centralization (SZGYF) causing inefficiency.
    *   **Ideology:** Money going to "Family Policy" (middle class) instead of "Child Protection" (poor/Roma).
*   **The Conclusion:** The government *doesn't care* about these children because they are politically irrelevant (Roma/Poor).
*   **Status:** **Insufficient.** It explains the *conditions* but not the *protection* of specific predators like Vásárhelyi or Juhász.

## Phase 2: The "Corruption & Nepotism" Hypothesis
**Source Material:** Initial reading of `bicske-szoloutca` transcripts + Bicske Pardon Scandal.

*   **The Core Idea:** Local elites are abusing the system, and their friends in high places are covering it up to avoid scandal.
*   **Key Drivers Identified:**
    *   **Feudalism:** Local "lords" (like the Bicske director) have powerful friends (Balog, Orbán family).
    *   **Quid Pro Quo:** "I help your wrestling club, you ignore my abuse."

*   **Status:** **Incomplete.** It explains the Bicske pardon but fails to explain the *systemic* nature of the silence (10 years at Szőlő utca) or the role of Intelligence Agencies.
